Contest Rules

The exam is a 25 question multiple choice test with five options labeled A through E. You will be given 75 minutes to complete the test.

Points are not calculated in a binary fasion: incorrect answers will be penalized. Specificially, a correct answer is worth 6 points, a blank answer is worth 1.5 points, and an incorrect answer is 0 points. Thus the maximum score that can be obtained is 150 points. Note that blank answers were worth 2 points in 2006 and 2007, and 2.5 points prior to 2006.

During the competition, you are only allowed to use the following items:

  • Writing utensils (e.g. pencils, pens, erasers)
  • Blank or lined scratch paper
  • Graph paper
  • Ruler
  • Compass
Note that exams prior to 2006 permit the use of calculators accepted on the SAT, though no problem requires the use of a calculator.

When using the mock testing interface, you cannot pause or exit the test or your progress will be lost. Your test will automatically be submitted when the timer runs out, but can also be submitted early.
